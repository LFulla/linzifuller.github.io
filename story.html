My story

From writing software to leading the people who do.

My journey as a developer made me wish technical leads stayed closer to the code. As an engineering manager and principal engineer, I bridge that gap.

Walking through a sprint review.

The long version

Technology has always fascinated me — the hardware, the systems, and the people who build them.

My passion for software began with games like Minecraft and League of Legends, sparking my curiosity about how software is built and optimized. In high school I competed in coding competitions, which led to a Lockheed Martin internship in 2018 — where I discovered an enthusiasm for engineering and leadership in equal measure.

I studied Computer Science and Mandarin at the University of Colorado Boulder, specializing in AI, ML, AR, VR, and automation. Though COVID-19 disrupted my degree, I stayed on at Lockheed Martin as a Software Engineer Intern, working on AR/VR applications before transitioning into Technical Project Management and leading college internship teams.

To formalize the leadership skills I'd been picking up on the job, I completed an 18-week Technical Project Management bootcamp at the University of Denver: Agile leadership, project lifecycles, cross-functional collaboration. I joined S&P Global as a Scrum Master, overseeing three software development teams on high-profile financial contracts. From there I became Engineering Manager at Returned.com, leading internal and outsourced teams in a fast-paced startup. Then I jumped at the chance to be a Principal Engineer at Mojang, where I ran the Forest and Warm Oceans biome teams — leading new game development and mentoring developers.

Today I'm Principal Engineering Manager at Accuris, running multiple cross-functional engineering teams and partnering with architecture and UI to make sure we ship what we promise. Across enterprise, startup, and games, the through-line has been the same: solve complex problems, lead well, and deliver work I can be proud of.
