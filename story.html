My journey as a developer made me wish
project leads had a technical background now,
as a technical project manager, I bridge that gap.

Technology has always fascinated me
whether it’s the hardware that powers computers or the complex systems that make them function seamlessly.

My passion for software development began with games like Minecraft and League of Legends, sparking my curiosity about how software is built and optimized. In high school, I competed in coding competitions, leading to a Lockheed Martin internship in 2018, where I discovered my enthusiasm for engineering and leadership.

I studied Computer Science and Mandarin at the University of Colorado Boulder, specializing in AI, ML, AR, VR, and automation. Though COVID-19 disrupted my degree completion, I remained committed to my career, spending four years at Lockheed Martin as a Software Engineer, working on AR/VR applications before transitioning into Technical Project Management, where I led college internship teams.

To formalize my expertise, I completed an 18-week Technical Project Management boot camp at Denver University, gaining skills in Agile leadership, project lifecycles, and cross-functional collaboration. I then joined S&P Global as a Scrum Master, overseeing three software development teams on high-profile financial contracts. Later, at Returned.com, I became an Engineering Manager, working with executives to drive product strategy and execution in a fast-paced startup.

With experience in enterprise and startup environments, I excel at solving complex problems, leading teams, and driving project success. Now, I’m eager to apply my technical expertise and leadership skills in a new Technical Project Management role to deliver innovation and impactful results.
